Puto

It may sound odd to Spanish speaking people, but Puto is a traditional Filipino steamed rice cake, usually enjoyed as a snack or accompaniment to savory dishes.

Puto is commonly made out of:

  • Rice Flour
  • Baking Powder
  • Sugar
  • Salt
  • Pandan
  • Coconut Milk (Optional)
  • Ube (Optional)

Suman

a Filipino sticky rice cake made with glutinous rice cooked in coconut milk, typically wrapped in banana leaves and steamed. It's often served with sugar or latik (coconut caramel). Suman is a popular dessert or snack, and there are variations in how it's prepared and wrapped across different regions of the Philippines.

The Main Ingredients are:

  • Sticky Rice
  • Coconut Milk
  • Sugar
  • Ginger (Optional)

Tuyo

"Tuyo" (Filipino for "dry") refers to a specific type of dried fish, particularly salted and dried herring, which is a popular food in the Philippines. It is a form of dried fish that is typically sun-dried or air-dried after being salted and eviscerated

Main Ingredients:

  • Fish
  • Salt
  • Heat
