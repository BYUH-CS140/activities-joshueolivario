The Pattern of Trust

My Activities Learn More Message

The first principle of the gospel of Jesus Christ is to have faith in Him. The topic of faith is one of the most discussed in many of our church meetings. Modern prophets have taught that having faith is not simply believing in God. Elder Dallin H. Oaks said, “Faith means trust—trust in God’s will, trust in His way of doing things, and trust in His timetable.”1

In this essay I will discuss the pattern of trusting in God . Please note that this pattern is not the only way of showing our trust in Him. This is based on the story of Jesus’s first miracle in John 2:2-10. The items of the pattern are communicating with God, listening to His answers, and acting upon the answers that we receive.

First, communicating with God shows that we trust Him. We can communicate with God in many ways. Elder Robert D. Hales taught, “when we want to speak to God, we pray. And when we want Him to speak to us, we search the scriptures…” 2. We show our trust in Him when we pray because we trust that He is there listening to the humble petitions of our hearts. And as we read the scriptures, we show Him that we are willing to receive instructions from Him.

Next, listening to His answers is another act of trust. We often see in the scriptures the word ‘Hearken’. President Nelson taught that the meaning of the word hearken is to listen with the intent to obey3. Hearkening to His words shows that we trust in His wisdom.

The prophet Joseph Smith is an example of trusting God when he acted upon the scriptures that he had read in James 1:5. A direct instruction from the Father as His answer to the prayer of the boy Joseph Smith says, “…Hear Him.”4. Joseph Smith’s listening with the intent to obey to the words of God resulted in the restoration of the fulness of his Gospel. Our intent to obey as we listen can also result into a miracle.

Lastly, we show our trust in Him when we act upon the answers that we receive. In the wedding feast, Mary instructed the servants to do whatever Jesus says. Then Jesus told the servants to fill the waterpots with water, and they did. And what happened next is His first miracle during His mortal ministry; turning water into wine. We might sometimes think that His answers are impossible. Nephi mentioned in 1 Nephi 17:41 that many have perished because they did not look upon the brazen serpent that the Lord instructed Moses to make. As we act to what He and His prophet say, miracles happen and our testimony grows.

"Faith means trust—trust in God’s will, trust in His way of doing things, and trust in His timetable." – Dallin H. Oaks1
